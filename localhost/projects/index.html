Projects

Our research group has many projects running in parallel. Below are a few of our main focus projects:

Computational Quantum Physics


 

To understand how the world works at the smallest scales, we need to understand the physics of quantum mechanics. In our group, we approach this understanding with high performance computations. Courses related to computational quantum mechanics include FYS4411, FYS-MENA4111 and FYS-KJM4480.

Read more →

Molecular dynamics

 

Above the scale of quantum mechanics, we find the physics of atoms and molecules. We study the behavior of systems on the molecular scale by the use of our own home-brewed code together with other state-of-the-art tools and simulation code. The FYS4460 course includes a good portion of molecular dynamics.

Computing cluster

 

We have our own in-house computing cluster which will be set up to meet the needs of our research group. This high performance computing cluster will also be available to students taking our courses in the future.

Read more →

Visualization

Interpreting complex systems and large data sets often boils down to creating good visualizations. We work hard with different visualization techniques and software to gain new insights from our simulations and create aesthetically pleasing graphics.
