About Morten Hjorth-Jensen

My research interests span most areas of computational physics and computational quantum mechanics in particular. The main focus has been and is on many-body methods for nuclear structure problems, but I have also done and continue to do research on Bose-Einstein condensation and the structure of quantum dots, in addition to studies of the mathematical properties of various many-body methods. In essence, my research can be summarized under the topic studies of algorithms and methods for solving Schroedinger’s equation or Dirac’s equation for many interacting particles.
