Morten Hjorth-Jensen
My research interests span most areas of computational physics and computational quantum mechanics in particular. The main focus has been and is on many-body methods for nuclear structure problems, but I have also done and continue to do research on Bose-Einstein condensation and the structure of quantum dots, in addition to studies of the mathematical properties of various many-body methods. In essence, my research can be summarized under the topic studies of algorithms and methods for solving Schroedinger's equation or Dirac's equation for many interacting particles.

Former PhD-students

Jørgen Kjoshagen Trømborg
My research project is titled "Modelling the onset of frictional sliding" and is a basic research project into how frictional interfaces go from being stuck to sliding. We use models that combine standard descriptions of elastic bulk deformation with simplified descriptions of the individual junctions that give rise to the friction force. Friction is ubiquitous and important, and we are part of the effort to build a bottom-up description capable of prediction frictional properties without resorting to empirical parametrization.
Gustav Baardsen
I finished a PhD within computational nuclear physics. In the PhD project, I implemented a new computational method to model a system called infinite nuclear matter.
